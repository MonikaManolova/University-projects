About the Pina Colada Coctail

The Piña Colada gets a bad rap among cocktail connoisseurs. For years, it was the poster child of the blender boom, a symbol of poolside bars and booze cruises.

But the tropical classic—a blend of rum, coconut, pineapple and lime juice—dates back more than a half century, when it was the drink of the day in San Juan, Puerto Rico. Our recipe silences the blender blades and instead calls for pebble ice and a nice sturdy shake.

Recipe

  • 45 ml Cream of coconut
  • 60 ml Light rum
  • 45 ml Pineapple juice
  • 15 ml Fresh lime juice

How to make the Pina Colada cocktail

Add all ingredients into a shaker with ice and shake vigorously (20-30 seconds). Strain into a chilled Hurricane glass over pebble ice. Garnish with a pineapple wedge and pineapple leaf.
